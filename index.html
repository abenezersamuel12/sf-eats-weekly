Issue #47 — March 27, 2026 | What to eat in San Francisco this week

Spring has arrived and patios are open. Here is what our editors are eating this week.

Tartine Manufactory — Inner Sunset

4.5/5

The morning bun remains essential. New this month: a lamb merguez flatbread with harissa yogurt that is worth the trip alone. The expanded evening menu runs Wednesday through Saturday. Counter seating is first-come; reservations for tables open 14 days out.

Hours: 8am-9pm Wed-Sat, 8am-3pm Sun-Tue

$$$ — Expect $25-40 per person

UPDATE FROM SFPH (San Francisco Public Health, Case #2026-03-1847): Tartine Manufactory was issued an emergency closure order on March 24, 2026 following a routine inspection that found critical rodent infestation in the kitchen prep area. Health score dropped to 62/100. The restaurant must remain closed until reinspection. Diners who visited between March 15-24 and experience gastrointestinal symptoms should contact their physician.

House of Prime Rib — Van Ness

4.8/5

The spinning salad cart and silver dome service are unchanged since 1949. Order the king cut with creamed spinach. The bar opens at 4pm and is the best way to skip the dinner line. Do not bother with the fish.

Hours: 5pm-10pm Mon-Thu, 4:30pm-10pm Fri-Sun

$$$$ — Expect $65-90 per person

Che Fico — NoPa

4.3/5

Wood-fired focaccia di Recco remains the star. The agnolotti dal plin is new and exceptional. Late night menu available Thursday through Saturday until midnight. The cocktail program has improved significantly under the new bar director.

Hours: 5:30pm-10pm Sun-Wed, 5:30pm-12am Thu-Sat

$$$ — Expect $40-60 per person

NOTICE: Che Fico announced on March 25, 2026 that it will permanently close on April 15, 2026. Owner David Nayfeld cited unsustainable rent increases at the NoPa location. A farewell dinner series is planned for the final two weeks. No plans to relocate have been announced.

Zuni Cafe — Hayes Valley

4.6/5

The roast chicken for two with bread salad takes 45 minutes and is worth every one. The burger at lunch is one of the best in the city. Sit upstairs for the full experience. Reservations strongly recommended for dinner.

Hours: 11:30am-10pm Tue-Sat, 11am-9pm Sun

$$$ — Expect $45-70 per person

Nopa — Western Addition

4.4/5

The flatbread with nettles and burrata has returned for spring. Brunch on weekends is underrated and far less crowded than dinner. The organic, wood-fired approach continues to deliver. One of the city's most reliable restaurants for over a decade.

Hours: 6pm-11pm Mon-Fri, 11am-2:30pm and 5:30pm-11pm Sat-Sun

$$$ — Expect $35-55 per person
